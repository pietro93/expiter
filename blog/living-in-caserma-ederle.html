Life in Caserma Ederle, Vicenza

Caserma Ederle, also known as Camp Ederle, is a United States Army base located in Vicenza, Italy.

It serves as the headquarters for the United States Army Africa (USARAF) and is under Italian military control.

The base includes facilities such as housing, a communications site, and a USAREUR ammunition supply point.

Vicenza, the city where Caserma Ederle is located, is known for its rich cultural heritage and proximity to Venice.

What is it like to live in Caserma Ederle?

Living in Caserma Ederle offers a unique blend of American and Italian cultures.

The base provides amenities such as a commissary, a PX (Post Exchange), schools, and recreational facilities.

Residents can enjoy the comfort of American-style living while being immersed in the beauty of Italian architecture and landscapes.

Outside the base, Vicenza offers a glimpse into Italy's history with its Palladian villas and the Teatro Olimpico.

It's also an excellent location for exploring other famous Italian cities and attractions.



What is there to do in Vicenza and its surroundings?

There are numerous activities to enjoy in Vicenza and the Veneto region:

Cultural exploration: Discover the architectural masterpieces of Andrea Palladio, including the Basilica Palladiana and Villa La Rotonda.

Venice trips: Take a short train ride to Venice to experience its canals, art, and history.

Outdoor activities: Enjoy hiking in the nearby Dolomites or relaxing at Lake Garda.

What are the pros and cons of living in Caserma Ederle and Vicenza?

Living in Caserma Ederle and Vicenza, like any other place, has its advantages and disadvantages that can vary based on individual preferences and circumstances.

Some of the pros include:

Cultural Richness: Vicenza is known for its rich cultural heritage, including architecture by the famous Renaissance architect Andrea Palladio.

Community: The military community at Caserma Ederle is tight-knit, offering a sense of belonging and support for service members and their families.

Location: Vicenza's location is ideal for exploring other parts of Italy and Europe, with good travel connections.

Some of the cons might be:

Language Barrier: While living in Italy, one might face challenges with the language barrier if not fluent in Italian.

Cost of Living: The cost of living can be high, especially if relying on imported goods or services tailored to expatriates.

Adjustment: Adapting to local customs and bureaucracy can take time and may be frustrating at times.
