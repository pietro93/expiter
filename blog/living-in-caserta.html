Living in Caserta

Caserta is a captivating city located in the Campania region of Italy. It is the capital of the province of Caserta and is situated about 36 kilometers north of Naples.

Known for its agricultural, commercial, and industrial significance, Caserta is nestled on the edge of the Campanian plain at the foot of the Campanian Subapennine mountain range.

The city is renowned for the 18th-century Bourbon Royal Palace of Caserta.

Living in Caserta offers a blend of rich history, stunning architecture, and authentic Italian culture.

Expats can enjoy the city’s vibrant atmosphere, warm and welcoming locals, and a variety of local customs and traditions.

Caserta is a major urban area in the Campania region, located further south than Rome and at a more northerly latitude than Naples.

With a population of about 72,516 inhabitants, Caserta spans over an area of 2,651.35 square kilometers.

As the regional capital of Campania, Caserta is a significant port city known for its historical and cultural attractions.

The city has a GDP of about €14.536 billion, making it one of the economically important cities in Italy.

The city is best known for the Bourbon Royal Palace of Caserta, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

What is Caserta like?

Caserta is a vibrant and diverse city with a strong cultural identity. It is home to people from different origins, religions, and backgrounds, who contribute to the city’s rich social and cultural life.

Caserta is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, boasting a wealth of monuments, churches, palaces, museums, and archaeological sites.

It is the birthplace of many famous artists, writers, musicians, and actors.

Some of the most famous cultural attractions in Caserta are the Bourbon Royal Palace of Caserta, the Belvedere of San Leucio, and the Carolino Aqueduct.

Climate

Caserta experiences a Mediterranean climate, with mild, rainy winters and hot, sunny summers.

The average temperature of the coldest month (January) is 8.4°C (47.1°F), and that of the warmest month (August) is 26.1°C (79.0°F).

The city receives an average of 1090 millimeters (42.9 inches) of precipitation per year.

Economy

Caserta is the capital city of the Campania region, and one of the main economic centers of southern Italy.

It has a diversified and dynamic economy, based on sectors such as industry, services, trade, tourism, and agriculture.

Caserta is also a major port and transport hub, connecting Italy with the rest of Europe.

Caserta is a cultural and educational center, hosting several universities, research institutes, museums, and festivals.

Caserta has a high quality of life, with a low cost of living, a rich gastronomy, and a mild climate.

Despite these advantages, the city faces challenges such as unemployment and social inequality.

The city authorities have implemented various policies and projects to improve the situation, focusing on innovation, sustainability, social inclusion, and territorial development.

Education

Caserta is home to several educational institutions. The city hosts the ‘New Education Forum’, an international platform that gathers prominent figures, leaders, and innovators in the field of education, training, and labor market.

The city also has schools like the ‘Social School Caserta’ and the ‘Planetario di Caserta’ which contribute to the educational landscape.

There are also initiatives like the ‘Evoluzione’ project, which provides ICT solutions, training courses, and educational supplies.


Cost of living in Caserta

The cost of living in Caserta is relatively lower compared to other Italian cities.

It is considered one of the most affordable cities in Italy and Europe. The estimated monthly costs for a family of four are €2,949 without rent, and for a single person, the estimated monthly costs are €1,267 without rent.

Rent in Caserta is also quite affordable, especially outside the city center.

For instance, a one-bedroom apartment in the city center costs about €488 per month, while a three-bedroom apartment costs about €832 per month.

The prices of food, transportation, utilities, and entertainment are also lower than the national average.

Is Caserta safe?

Caserta is generally considered a safe city. However, like any other city, it has its share of crime. The crime rate in Caserta is moderate, with crimes such as theft, robbery, and vandalism being the most common.

Safety during daylight is rated as high, while safety during the night is rated as low.

It’s always advisable for residents and visitors to be vigilant and take necessary precautions.

Advantages of Living in Caserta

Caserta is a captivating city located in the Campania region of Italy. It is known for its agricultural, commercial, and industrial significance.

The city offers a variety of opportunities in industry, trade, tourism, and innovation2.

It is also a cultural and educational center, hosting several universities, research institutes, museums, and festivals.

In general, Caserta has a friendly and relaxed atmosphere, with a lot of music, art, and traditions, and a strong sense of identity.

Moreover, Caserta is strategically located near some of the most beautiful and charming places in Italy, such as Naples.

Caserta is a diverse city, where you can meet people from different backgrounds and origins.

Italian is the main language spoken here, but you can also find some people who speak English, French, or German, especially in the business and academic sectors.

The city has a moderate cost of living, which makes it affordable and attractive for many people, especially compared to other Italian cities.

In terms of weather, Caserta has a Mediterranean climate, with mild, rainy winters and hot, sunny summers, which can make you feel comfortable and cheerful.

Disadvantages of Living in Caserta

Living in Caserta can be challenging. The city, like any other, has its share of crime. The crime rate in Caserta is moderate, with crimes such as theft, robbery, and vandalism being the most common.

Residents and visitors may encounter problems with traffic, parking, and public transport.

Some areas of the city can be unsafe and violent, especially where theft, robbery, and vandalism are common.

It can also be somewhat noisy and polluted, especially near the industrial zones.

Another issue is that Caserta is not very open to foreigners, and the cultural differences can be a challenge, especially if you don’t speak Italian.

You can also struggle to adapt to the local customs and habits. In addition to that, compared to other parts of Italy, healthcare in Caserta is somewhat lacking.


FAQs

What is Caserta like for foreigners?

Caserta, a city steeped in historical allure and equipped with contemporary conveniences, offers expatriates a satisfactory standard of living.

While the healthcare and education sectors could benefit from enhancements, the city maintains a moderate level of safety, with crime rates marginally lower than the national average.

The vibrant nightlife in Caserta presents an engaging atmosphere for expatriates, although not as vibrant as in larger cities nearby such as Rome or Naples.

What is Caserta like for students?

Caserta is home to the Royal Palace, a UNESCO World Heritage site, which offers free access to students of advanced training courses of the Schools of the Ministry, teachers and students enrolled in academies of fine arts or corresponding institutions in the European Union234.

This provides a rich cultural experience for students living in Caserta.

What is Caserta like for women?

Women may face more challenges in Caserta compared to other provinces in Italy.

Unfortunately, there are higher rates of discrimination and violence against women compared to larger, more open-minded cities in Northern Italy.

Women may struggle with issues like sexism, catcalling, unemployment and the presence of a gender gap, among other things.

What is Caserta like for LGBTQ people?

Caserta has an increasingly supportive environment for the LGBTQ+ community.

The city is home to Rain Arcigay Caserta, a volunteer association that promotes and defends LGBTQ+ rights5.

The association provides psychological and legal support for victims of homophobic incidents and helps those struggling to accept people with different orientations or identities5.

They also advocate for equal marriage rights and civil unions5. In 2021, they inaugurated the “QTC Checkpoint”, a community-based screening service for HIV, HCV, and Syphilis tests6.
