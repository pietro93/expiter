Living in Perugia

Perugia is a charming city located in the Umbria region of central Italy, known for its rich history, vibrant culture, and stunning architecture.



The city is situated on a hilltop, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside.



Perugia is a must-visit destination for anyone interested in exploring Italy's cultural heritage..





Perugia is located about 102 miles north of Rome and 92 miles southeast of Florence.



It covers a high hilltop and part of the valleys around the area.



The city has a history dating back to the Etruscan civilization, with evidence of this ancient heritage still visible in the well-preserved Etruscan walls that encircle parts of the city.



Perugia is home to the University of Perugia, founded in 1308, as well as several other institutions, making it a hub for education and intellectual pursuits..

What is Perugia like?

Perugia boasts a rich tradition of art and architecture, with notable landmarks such as the Rocca Paolina, Ipogeo dei Volumni, and Etruscan town walls.



The city is also famous for its Renaissance frescoes, including works by Pietro Perugino and Raphael.



Perugia hosts various annual festivals and events, including the Eurochocolate Festival, the Umbria Jazz Festival, and the International Journalism Festival.



These events bring the city to life with music, art, and delicious treats.



Perugia is renowned for its chocolate, particularly Perugina, which is famous for its Baci chocolates.



The city even hosts chocolate-making classes and festivals dedicated to chocolate.



Perugian cuisine is known for its hearty dishes, such as pappardelle al cinghiale (wild boar pasta) and pici all'aglione (hand-rolled pasta with tomato sauce)..

Disadvantages of Living in Perugia

Perugia can be cool and windy to live in.



You can face difficulties in finding affordable housing, especially in the city center, where the prices are high and the availability is low.



You can also encounter problems with the language and culture, as Italian is not the only language spoken and the locals can be reserved and distant.



The city can be isolated and less connected, with fewer flights and trains, which can affect your travel and mobility.



Perugia is not very diverse, and the expat community is small and scattered.



You can also have trouble finding work and opportunities, as the economy is not very stable and the competition is high, especially for foreigners.



Moreover, the city can have a complex and controversial history, which can create some political and territorial tensions, especially with its neighboring countries.



In addition to that, compared to other parts of Italy, Perugia can have a less lively and cheerful atmosphere, which can make it dull and boring for some people..
