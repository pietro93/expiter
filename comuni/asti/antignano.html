Antignano, Piedmont, Italy

NameAntignano
ProvinceAsti
RegionPiedmont
Population964
Density89pp/km²
Altitude260m
Climate ZoneE

Antignano is a municipality of 964 inhabitants located in the Asti province in the Italian region of Piedmont in Northern Italy.

It has a population density of 89 people per km² and an altitude of 260 metres above the sea level.

Antignano accounts for about 0.45% of the total population in the province of Asti and about 0.00160% of the overall population of Italy as of 2022.

Climate

Antignano is classified as a Climate Zone E, which means it is a fairly chill town. The local climate is characterized by winters which are long and cold and summers which are dry and very hot.

There are frequent precipitations in autumn and spring, and fog is not uncommon.

The province of Asti experiences on average 66.41 days of hot temperatures (over 30°C) and 18.10 cold temperature days (<5°C) per year. It rains (or snows) around 75.96 days per year. There are 52.11 foggy days throughout the year. Antignano receives around 7.4 hours of sunshine per day on average.
