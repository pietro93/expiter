About San michele di serino

San michele di serino is a municipality of 2.436 inhabitants located in the Avellino province in the Italian region of Campania in Southern Italy.

It has a population density of 545pp/km² people per km² and an altitude of 364m metres above the sea level.

San michele di serino accounts for about 0.58% of the total population in the province of Avellino and about 0.00404% of the overall population of Italy as of 2022.

Bari was the seat of the Kingdom of Naples, the Kingdom of the Two Sicilies, and the Kingdom of Italy. It was also a strategic port for trade and commerce in the Mediterranean.

Bari was bombed by the Allies, liberated by the Polish Army, and rebuilt after World War II. It is now a thriving city and a popular tourist destination.

Climate

San michele di serino is classified as a Climate Zone D, which means it is a temperate town by Italian standards. The local climate is characterized by short, mild winters with hot and somewhat windy summers and overall little precipitation.

The province of Avellino experiences on average 63.91 days of hot temperatures (over 30°C) and 6.41 cold temperature days (<5°C) per year.

Precipitation in the form of rain or snow occurs around 100.80 days per year. Fog is rarely an issue, with little to no foggy days throughout the year.

The municipality of San michele di serino enjoys approximately 7.5 hours of sunshine per day. This means that the town experiences an average of 304.17 sunny days in a year.
