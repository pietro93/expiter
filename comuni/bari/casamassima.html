Casamassima is a municipality of 19.401 inhabitants located in the Bari province in the Italian region of Apulia in Southern Italy. It has a population density of 247 people per km² and an altitude of 223 metres above the sea level.

Casamassima accounts for about 1.55% of the total population in the province of Bari and about 0.03220% of the overall population of Italy as of 2022.

Climate

Casamassima is classified as a Climate Zone D, which means it is a temperate town by Italian standards. The local climate is characterized by cold winters and hot but windy summers, with somewhat frequent rainfall spread throughout the year.

The province of Bari experiences on average 62.50 days of hot temperatures (over 30°C) and 0.89 cold temperature days (<5°C) per year. It rains (or snows) around 71.76 days per year. There are 5.21 foggy days throughout the year. Casamassima receives around 8.5 hours of sunshine per day on average.
