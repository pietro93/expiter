About Gildone

Gildone is a municipality of 750 inhabitants located in the Campobasso province in the Italian region of Molise in Southern Italy.

It has a population density of 25pp/km² people per km² and an altitude of 608m metres above the sea level.

Gildone accounts for about 0.34% of the total population in the province of Campobasso and about 0.00124% of the overall population of Italy as of 2022.

Bari also has a lively waterfront, a modern city center, and a historic core, known as Bari Vecchia.

Some of the most characteristic aspects of Bari are the cult of Saint Nicholas, the patron saint of the city, the tradition of the orecchiette pasta, and the dialect of the Barese people.

Bari was founded by the Peucetians in the 8th century BC. It was conquered by the Romans, the Byzantines, the Lombards, the Saracens, the Normans, the Swabians, the Angevins, the Aragonese, and the Bourbons.

Bari was the seat of the Kingdom of Naples, the Kingdom of the Two Sicilies, and the Kingdom of Italy. It was also a strategic port for trade and commerce in the Mediterranean.

Bari was bombed by the Allies, liberated by the Polish Army, and rebuilt after World War II. It is now a thriving city and a popular tourist destination.

Climate

Gildone is classified as a Climate Zone E, which means it is a fairly chill town. The local climate is characterized by long and cold winters with frequent snowfall in mountaineous areas, and somewhat warm and dry summers.

The province of Campobasso experiences on average 44.19 days of hot temperatures (over 30°C) and 22.39 cold temperature days (<5°C) per year.

Precipitation in the form of rain or snow occurs around 86.28 days per year. Fog is rarely an issue, with little to no foggy days throughout the year.

The municipality of Gildone enjoys approximately 7.4 hours of sunshine per day. This means that the town experiences an average of 300.11 sunny days in a year.
