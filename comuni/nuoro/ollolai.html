About Ollolai

Ollolai is a municipality of 1.190 inhabitants located in the Nuoro province in the Italian region of Sardinia in Southern Italy.

It has a population density of 44pp/km² people per km² and an altitude of 920m metres above the sea level.

Ollolai accounts for about 0.57% of the total population in the province of Nuoro and about 0.00197% of the overall population of Italy as of 2022.

About Ollolai

Ollolai is a village in the heart of Barbagia, with a rebellious and proud spirit.

It has several historical and cultural sites, such as the Church of San Michele Arcangelo, the Church of Sant’Antonio, and the Church of San Basilio.

Ollolai is also famous for its traditional crafts, such as baskets, textiles, and cheese.

Ollolai has ancient origins, dating back to the prehistoric and Roman times.

It was the main town of the Barbagia di Ollolai, a feudal territory in the Giudicale period.

Ollolai resisted the invasions of the Byzantines, the Aragonese, and the Savoyards, preserving its autonomy and identity.

Ollolai was also the birthplace of Michele Columbu, a leader of the Sardinian nationalist movement.

Climate

Ollolai is classified as a Climate Zone E, which means it is a fairly chill town. The local climate is characterized by short, mild winters and long, hot and dry summers. Rainfall is mostly concentrated to the winter period.

The province of Nuoro experiences on average 68.09 days of hot temperatures (over 30°C) and 5.79 cold temperature days (<5°C) per year.

Precipitation in the form of rain or snow occurs around 86.88 days per year. Fog is rarely an issue, with little to no foggy days throughout the year.

The municipality of Ollolai enjoys approximately 7.7 hours of sunshine per day. This means that the town experiences an average of 312.28 sunny days in a year.
