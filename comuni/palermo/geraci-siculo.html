About Geraci siculo

Geraci siculo is a municipality of 1.707 inhabitants located in the Palermo province in the Italian region of Sicily in Southern Italy.

It has a population density of 15pp/km² people per km² and an altitude of 1.077m metres above the sea level.

Geraci siculo accounts for about 0.14% of the total population in the province of Palermo and about 0.00283% of the overall population of Italy as of 2022.

Bari also has a lively waterfront, a modern city center, and a historic core, known as Bari Vecchia.

Bari was the seat of the Kingdom of Naples, the Kingdom of the Two Sicilies, and the Kingdom of Italy. It was also a strategic port for trade and commerce in the Mediterranean.

Bari was bombed by the Allies, liberated by the Polish Army, and rebuilt after World War II. It is now a thriving city and a popular tourist destination.

Climate

Geraci siculo is classified as a Climate Zone E, which means it is a fairly chill town. The local climate is characterized by short, mild winters and long, hot and dry summers. Rainfall is mostly concentrated to the winter period.

The province of Palermo experiences on average 97.51 days of hot temperatures (over 30°C) and 0.00 cold temperature days (<5°C) per year.

Precipitation in the form of rain or snow occurs around 83.40 days per year. Fog is rarely an issue, with little to no foggy days throughout the year.

The municipality of Geraci siculo enjoys approximately 8.6 hours of sunshine per day. This means that the town experiences an average of 348.78 sunny days in a year.
