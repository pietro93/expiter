About Siena

Siena is known for its historic sites such as Piazza del Campo and Duomo di Siena.

Other attractions include Centro Storico di Siena and Torre del Mangia.

Siena lies in the picturesque Tuscan hills south of Florence.

It began to prosper in the 12th century and reached its artistic zenith in the 13th and 14th centuries.

Climate

Siena is classified as a Climate Zone D, which means it is a temperate town by Italian standards. The local climate is characterized by short, mild winters with hot and somewhat windy summers and overall little precipitation.
