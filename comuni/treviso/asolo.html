About Asolo

Asolo is a municipality of 8.935 inhabitants located in the Treviso province in the Italian region of Veneto in Northern Italy.

It has a population density of 352pp/km² people per km² and an altitude of 190m metres above the sea level.

Asolo accounts for about 1.01% of the total population in the province of Treviso and about 0.01483% of the overall population of Italy as of 2022.

Bari also has a lively waterfront, a modern city center, and a historic core, known as Bari Vecchia.

Bari was founded by the Peucetians in the 8th century BC. It was conquered by the Romans, the Byzantines, the Lombards, the Saracens, the Normans, the Swabians, the Angevins, the Aragonese, and the Bourbons.

Bari was the seat of the Kingdom of Naples, the Kingdom of the Two Sicilies, and the Kingdom of Italy. It was also a strategic port for trade and commerce in the Mediterranean.

Bari was bombed by the Allies, liberated by the Polish Army, and rebuilt after World War II. It is now a thriving city and a popular tourist destination.

Climate

Asolo is classified as a Climate Zone E, which means it is a fairly chill town. The local climate is characterized by winters which are long and cold and summers which are dry and very hot. There are frequent precipitations in autumn and spring, and fog is not uncommon.

The province of Treviso experiences on average 73.41 days of hot temperatures (over 30°C) and 7.71 cold temperature days (<5°C) per year.

Precipitation in the form of rain or snow occurs around 102.72 days per year. There are 21.91 foggy days throughout the year.

The municipality of Asolo enjoys approximately 7.6 hours of sunshine per day. This means that the town experiences an average of 308.22 sunny days in a year.
