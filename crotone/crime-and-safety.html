Crime and Safety

The province of Crotone ranks 70 out of 106 for safety according to our data, with a safety score of 5.

There were a total of 2994.178 official reports of crime per 100,000 inhabitants in the province in 2023.

This is in line with the average number of reported crimes per 100,000 inhabitants across all Italian provinces.

This suggests that the crime rate in Crotone is typical for Italian provinces.

Thefts and Robberies

In the province of Crotone, there were 36.4 instances of thefts with dexterity (thefts committed with special skill, superior to that normally used by the common thief), 6.174 instances of snatch thefts (thefts committed by someone who takes possession of another's movable property by snatching it from the person's hand or body), 53.71 car thefts, and 56.797 home burglaries per 100,000 inhabitants in 2023.
