Overview

This page provides a data-driven overview of crime and safety in Bolzano, in the Trentino-Alto Adige region. Based on official 2023 statistics, Bolzano is about average compared to other Italian provinces in terms of safety, ranking 68 out of 106 with a safety score of 5/10.

Below you will find details on thefts, violent crimes, organized crime, drug-related offenses, and accidents, along with answers to common questions about safety in Bolzano.

Crime and Safety

The province of Bolzano ranks 68 out of 106 for safety according to our data, with a safety score of 5. There were a total of 3048.754 official reports of crime per 100,000 inhabitants in the province in 2023. This is in line with the average number of reported crimes per 100,000 inhabitants across all Italian provinces. This suggests that the crime rate in Bolzano is typical for Italian provinces.



Thefts and Robberies

In the province of Bolzano, there were 33 instances of thefts with dexterity (thefts committed with special skill, superior to that normally used by the common thief), 10.126 instances of snatch thefts (thefts committed by someone who takes possession of another's movable property by snatching it from the person's hand or body), 14.064 car thefts, and 188.273 home burglaries per 100,000 inhabitants in 2023.

Violent Crimes

In the province of Bolzano, there were 18.75 instances of violent crimes (homicides and sexual assaults) per 100,000 inhabitants in 2023.
