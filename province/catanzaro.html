Overview

The province of Catanzaro is the 59th largest Italian province by population with 358.316 people, located in the Calabria region.

The city of Catanzaro, with a population of approximately 91,492 residents, is the capital of the Calabria region in southern Italy.

Located on a hilltop overlooking the Gulf of Squillace, Catanzaro offers stunning panoramic views of the surrounding landscapes.

The city is known for its historic center, with medieval and Baroque architecture, including the Cathedral of Santa Maria Assunta and the Norman Castle.

Catanzaro is also a cultural hub, hosting events such as the Catanzaro Jazz Festival and the International Folklore Festival.

The larger Catanzaro metropolitan area comprises 80 towns (comuni) and covers an area of 241.545 km2.

The population density is 148 inhabitants per km2, making it somewhat densely populated.

The male to female ratio is 1.00:1.06.

Catanzaro is a city of 86,000 people (2020) that is the capital of the Calabria.

During the summer months, the Ionian coast from Catanzaro to Soverato is an important tourist attraction, especially for the youth.

There are several important structures in the coastal districts of the city and the towns of Copanello and Soverato.
