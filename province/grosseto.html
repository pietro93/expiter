Overview

The province of Grosseto is the 85th largest Italian province by population with 221.629 people, located in the Tuscany region.



The larger Grosseto metropolitan area comprises 28 towns (comuni) and covers an area of 450.312 km2.



The population density is 49 inhabitants per km2, making it sparcely populated.



The male to female ratio is 1.00:1.08.

Climate

The province of Grosseto receives on average 261 hours of sunshine per month, or 8.7 hours of sunshine per day.NaN This is 10.59% more than the average for Italy and 9.16% more than the average for the region of Tuscany.

Throughout the year, it rains on average 6.13 days per month, which is below average for an Italian province.

During the autumn and winter season, there are usually 0.41 days per month with fog and 0.35 cold days per month with perceived temperatures below 3°C.



In the summer, there are on average 28.67 hot days per month with perceived temperatures above 30°C.

Cost of Living

The average monthly income in Grosseto is around 1332.26€, which is lower than the average for Italy.

The estimated cost of living is around 1882.68€ per month for an individual or 2773.13€ per month for a family of 4.





The cost for renting a small apartment (2-3 bedrooms) in a main city area is around 1165.27€ per month.

Overall, Grosseto is expensive compared to other Italian provinces.

Living in Grosseto is around 14.09% more expensive than the average of all Italian provinces.

.

The province of Grosseto is overall very safe for expats.



As of 2021, there are an average of 3075.8 reported crimes per 100k inhabitants.

This is respectively 7.41% less driving accidents than average and -58.14% less work accidents than average.
