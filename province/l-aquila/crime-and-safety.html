Crime and Safety

The province of L'Aquila ranks 11 out of 106 for safety according to our data, with a safety score of 8.

There were a total of 2279.985 official reports of crime per 100,000 inhabitants in the province in 2023.

This is in line with the average number of reported crimes per 100,000 inhabitants across all Italian provinces.

This suggests that the crime rate in L'Aquila is typical for Italian provinces.

Thefts and Robberies

In the province of L'Aquila, there were 20.2 instances of thefts with dexterity (thefts committed with special skill, superior to that normally used by the common thief), 4.875 instances of snatch thefts (thefts committed by someone who takes possession of another's movable property by snatching it from the person's hand or body), 37.263 car thefts, and 141.041 home burglaries per 100,000 inhabitants in 2023.

Violent Crimes

In the province of L'Aquila, there were 10.45 instances of violent crimes (homicides and sexual assaults) per 100,000 inhabitants in 2023.

This is in line with the average number of violent crimes per 100,000 inhabitants across all Italian provinces.

Mafia Activity

L'Aquila, the capital city of the Abruzzo region, has been targeted by various mafia organizations.

The Nigerian mafia has been active in the city, leading to police operations and arrests.

L'Aquila was also linked to Matteo Messina Denaro, a prominent Sicilian mafia boss who was hospitalized in the city before his death.

Additionally, the "mafia dei pascoli" has infiltrated the local agricultural and livestock sectors, impacting businesses and the regional economy.

These criminal activities highlight the presence and influence of organized crime groups in L'Aquila, posing challenges for the city and the Abruzzo region.

Accidents

In the province of L'Aquila, there were 13 instances of fatal and permanent disability accidents per 10,000 employees, and 2.351 road accident deaths per 10,000 residents in 2023.

Workplace accidents are in line with the national average average.

Road accident deaths are in line with the national average.

This suggests that the rate of road accident deaths in L'Aquila is typical for Italian provinces.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is L'Aquila safe to live?

Living in L'Aquila is considered relatively safe, despite challenges related to quality of life and security.

The city offers a lower cost of living compared to other Italian cities, making it more affordable for students and those seeking a peaceful environment and connection with nature.

Despite the presence of organized crime and corruption issues, violent crimes rarely involve expats or tourists.

Taking normal precautions, as in any other city, by keeping belongings safe and avoiding isolated areas at night is advisable.

Despite the challenges, L'Aquila offers a unique experience enriched by its history, culture, and breathtaking landscapes, making it a special place for those who choose to live there.
