Overview

This page provides a data-driven overview of crime and safety in Latina, in the Lazio region. Based on official 2023 statistics, Latina is one of the less safe Italian provinces, ranking 91 out of 106 with a safety score of 3/10.

Below you will find details on thefts, violent crimes, organized crime, drug-related offenses, and accidents, along with answers to common questions about safety in Latina.

Crime and Safety

The province of Latina ranks 91 out of 106 for safety according to our data, with a safety score of 3. There were a total of 3369.087 official reports of crime per 100,000 inhabitants in the province in 2023. This is in line with the average number of reported crimes per 100,000 inhabitants across all Italian provinces. This suggests that the crime rate in Latina is typical for Italian provinces.



Thefts and Robberies

In the province of Latina, there were 28.1 instances of thefts with dexterity (thefts committed with special skill, superior to that normally used by the common thief), 6.714 instances of snatch thefts (thefts committed by someone who takes possession of another's movable property by snatching it from the person's hand or body), 105.477 car thefts, and 194.7 home burglaries per 100,000 inhabitants in 2023.

Violent Crimes

In the province of Latina, there were 15.19 instances of violent crimes (homicides and sexual assaults) per 100,000 inhabitants in 2023. This is in line with the average number of violent crimes per 100,000 inhabitants across all Italian provinces. This suggests that the violent crime rate in Latina is typical for Italian provinces.



Organized Crime & Drug-related Crimes

In the province of Latina, there were 19.435 instances of extortions, 5.3 instances of money laundering, and 35.336 drug-related crimes (such as dealing, production, etc.) per 100,000 inhabitants in 2023.

Extortions are in line with the average for provinces in Italy. This suggests that the rate of extortions in Latina is typical for Italian provinces.

Accidents

In the province of Latina, there were 9.2 instances of fatal and permanent disability accidents per 10,000 employees, and 4.764 road accident deaths per 10,000 residents in 2023.
