Overview

The province of Massa-Carrara is the 94th largest Italian province by population with 194.878 people, located in the Tuscany region.



The larger Massa-Carrara metropolitan area comprises 17 towns (comuni) and covers an area of 115.468 km2.



The population density is 169 inhabitants per km2, making it somewhat densely populated.



The male to female ratio is 1.00:1.08.

Climate

The province of Massa-Carrara receives on average 240 hours of sunshine per month, or 8 hours of sunshine per day.NaN This is 1.69% more than the average for Italy and 0.38% more than the average for the region of Tuscany.

Throughout the year, it rains on average 7.75 days per month, which is an ordinary amount of precipitation for an Italian province.

During the autumn and winter season, there are usually 0.5 days per month with fog and 0.41 cold days per month with perceived temperatures below 3°C.



In the summer, there are on average 23.48 hot days per month with perceived temperatures above 30°C.

Cost of Living

The average monthly income in Massa-Carrara is around 1601.84€, which is close to the average for Italy.

The estimated cost of living is around 1976.02€ per month for an individual or 2929.54€ per month for a family of 4.





The cost for renting a small apartment (2-3 bedrooms) in a main city area is around 1162.22€ per month.

Overall, Massa-Carrara is expensive compared to other Italian provinces.

Living in Massa-Carrara is around 19.75% more expensive than the average of all Italian provinces.
