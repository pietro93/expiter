Overview

The province of Modena is the 24th largest Italian province by population with 705.393 people, located in the Emilia-Romagna region.



The larger Modena metropolitan area comprises 47 towns (comuni) and covers an area of 268.802 km2.



The population density is 262 inhabitants per km2, making it somewhat densely populated.



The male to female ratio is 1.00:1.05.

Climate

The province of Modena receives on average 231 hours of sunshine per month, or 7.7 hours of sunshine per day.NaN This is 2.12% less than the average for Italy and 0.72% less than the average for the region of Emilia-Romagna.

Throughout the year, it rains on average 6.85 days per month, which is below average for an Italian province.

During the autumn and winter season, there are usually 8.55 days per month with fog and 4.35 cold days per month with perceived temperatures below 3°C.



In the summer, there are on average 24.3 hot days per month with perceived temperatures above 30°C.

Cost of Living

The average monthly income in Modena is around 2145.88€, which is higher than the average for Italy.

The estimated cost of living is around 2070.76€ per month for an individual or 3115.31€ per month for a family of 4.





The cost for renting a small apartment (2-3 bedrooms) in a main city area is around 1022.37€ per month.

Overall, Modena is expensive compared to other Italian provinces.

Living in Modena is around 25.49% more expensive than the average of all Italian provinces.

.

Crime and Safety

The province of Modena is overall moderately safe for expats.



As of 2021, there are an average of 3722.4 reported crimes per 100k inhabitants.



This is 31.27% higher than the national average.

There have been around 0.7 deadly road accidents and 12.5 serious work-related injuries per 10k people in Modena.



This is respectively 29.63% more driving accidents than average and 3.10% less work accidents than average.

Car theft is reportedly 42.46% lower than average with only 40.58 cases per 100k inhabitants.
