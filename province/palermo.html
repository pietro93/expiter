Overview

The province of Palermo is the 7th largest Italian province by population with 1.245.826 people, located in the Sicily region.

The city of Palermo is the capital of Sicily, an island region of Italy.

It is a vibrant and lively city with a fascinating history that spans several centuries.

Palermo is known for its bustling street markets, vibrant neighborhoods, and architectural landmarks such as the Palermo Cathedral and the Norman Palace.

The city offers a mix of different cultures and influences, reflected in its diverse cuisine and vibrant street life.

The larger Palermo metropolitan area comprises 82 towns (comuni) and covers an area of 4992 km2.

The population density is 249 inhabitants per km2, making it somewhat densely populated. The male to female ratio is 1.00:1.07.

Climate

The province of Palermo receives on average 258 hours of sunshine per month, or 8.6 hours of sunshine per day.

This is 9.32% more than the average for Italy and 0.64% less than the average for the region of Sicily.

Throughout the year, it rains on average 6.95 days per month, which is below average for an Italian province.

During the autumn and winter season, there are usually 0.23 days per month with fog and 0 cold days per month with perceived temperatures below 3°C. In the summer, there are on average 28.44 hot days per month with perceived temperatures above 30°C.

Cost of Living

The average monthly income in Palermo is around 1394.04€, which is lower than the average for Italy.

The estimated cost of living is around 1375.46€ per month for an individual or 2064.59€ per month for a family of 4.

The cost for renting a small apartment (2-3 bedrooms) in a main city area is around 686€ per month.

Overall, Palermo is affordable compared to other Italian provinces.

Living in Palermo is around 16.64% cheaper than the average of all Italian provinces.

Quality of Life

Healthcare

Healthcare in Palermo is above average. For every 10k inhabitants, there are around 3.13 pharmacies, 9.68 general practitioners and 37.7 specialized doctors per 10k inhabitants.

Average life expectancy in Palermo is 81.3 years of age.



Education

Palermo has a lower-than-average percentage of high school graduates, around 53.6%; and a lower-than-average percentage of university graduates, around 20.7%.

The average number of completed years of schooling for people over 25 is 10.02, which is not far from the national average of 10.44.

There is one university in the province.



Leisure

Palermo offers a lively and authentic nightlife experience. While not as large as some other cities, it has a vibrant atmosphere and plenty of options for an enjoyable evening. Travelers can explore the city's lively squares and narrow streets, discovering charming bars and cozy taverns.

Palermo's nightlife scene is known for its friendly ambiance, where locals and visitors can mingle and enjoy the city's warm hospitality.

Overall, Palermo has pretty good nightlife with 1.65 bars and 3.06 restaurants per 10k inhabitants.



Crime and Safety

The province of Palermo is overall moderately safe for expats. As of 2021, there are an average of 3137.2 reported crimes per 100k inhabitants. This is 10.63% higher than the national average.

There have been around 0.3 deadly road accidents and 12.3 serious work-related injuries per 10k people in Palermo. This is respectively 44.44% less driving accidents than average and 4.65% less work accidents than average.

Car theft is reportedly 196.03% higher than average with 208.79 cases per 100k inhabitants.

Reports of house thefts are 43.66% lower than average with 98.61 cases per 100k inhabitants.

Cases of robbery are not totally uncommon, around 101.26% higher than average with 44.56 reports per 100k inhabitants.

Palermo has seen improvements in safety and security in recent years.

While the city has dealt with organized crime in the past, efforts have been made to enhance safety for residents and visitors.

Tourists should be cautious of pickpocketing and bag snatching, particularly in crowded areas and tourist attractions.

Women should take extra precautions when traveling alone at night.

It is advisable to stay in well-populated areas and use reputable transportation. Palermo has a relatively higher crime rate compared to other cities in Italy, including occasional instances of violent crimes. However, the majority of visitors enjoy their time in Palermo without any issues. Road accidents and reckless driving can occur, so it is important to exercise caution when on the roads.


Transport

Public transport in Palermo is lacking, and traffic is below average.

There are on average 0.61 active vehicles per person, against a national average of 0.66.

Around 0.19km per 10k inhabitants of the main city in Palermo consist of bicycle lanes. This makes Palermo not very bike-friendly.
