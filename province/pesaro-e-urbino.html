Overview

The province of Pesaro e Urbino is the 60th largest Italian province by population with 351.303 people, located in the Marche region.



The larger Pesaro e Urbino metropolitan area comprises 50 towns (comuni) and covers an area of 250.760 km2.

The population density is 140 inhabitants per km2, making it somewhat densely populated. The male to female ratio is 1.00:1.06.

Climate

The province of Pesaro e Urbino receives on average 237 hours of sunshine per month, or 7.9 hours of sunshine per day.

This is 0.42% more than the average for Italy and 0.51% more than the average for the region of Marche.

Throughout the year, it rains on average 6.79 days per month, which is below average for an Italian province.

During the autumn and winter season, there are usually 3.76 days per month with fog and 3.21 cold days per month with perceived temperatures below 3°C. In the summer, there are on average 11.84 hot days per month with perceived temperatures above 30°C.

Cost of Living

The average monthly income in Pesaro e Urbino is around 1677.64€, which is close to the average for Italy.

The estimated cost of living is around 1777.47€ per month for an individual or 2658.62€ per month for a family of 4.

The cost for renting a small apartment (2-3 bedrooms) in a main city area is around 766€ per month.

Overall, Pesaro e Urbino is expensive compared to other Italian provinces.

Living in Pesaro e Urbino is around 7.72% more expensive than the average of all Italian provinces.

Quality of Life

Healthcare

Healthcare in Pesaro e Urbino is below average. For every 10k inhabitants, there are around 3.56 pharmacies, 8.98 general practitioners and 21.2 specialized doctors per 10k inhabitants.

Average life expectancy in Pesaro e Urbino is very high at 82.3 years of age.



Education

Pesaro e Urbino has a higher-than-average percentage of high school graduates, around 65%; and a lower-than-average percentage of university graduates, around 24.9%.

The average number of completed years of schooling for people over 25 is 10.64, which is not far from the national average of 10.44.

There is one university in the province.



Leisure



Overall, Pesaro e Urbino has somewhat decent nightlife with 2.84 bars and 4.36 restaurants per 10k inhabitants.



Crime and Safety

The province of Pesaro e Urbino is overall very safe for expats. As of 2021, there are an average of 2118.2 reported crimes per 100k inhabitants. This is 25.30% lower than the national average.

There have been around 0.6 deadly road accidents and 16.8 serious work-related injuries per 10k people in Pesaro e Urbino. This is respectively 11.11% more driving accidents than average and -30.23% less work accidents than average.

Car theft is reportedly 74.20% lower than average with only 18.2 cases per 100k inhabitants.

Reports of house thefts are 17.93% lower than average with 143.64 cases per 100k inhabitants.

Cases of robbery are uncommon with 143.64 reported cases per 100k inhabitants, about 26.65% less the national average.

Pesaro e Urbino is considered one of the safest provinces in Italy with a low crime rate.

The province maintains a secure environment for its residents and visitors. Instances of crime, particularly violent crime, are relatively rare in Pesaro e Urbino. The local authorities prioritize public safety and employ measures to prevent and combat crime. As with any area, it is recommended to exercise basic precautions, such as safeguarding personal belongings and being mindful of your surroundings. Road accidents are infrequent, but it is essential to follow traffic regulations and drive responsibly.


Transport

Public transport in Pesaro e Urbino is lacking, and traffic is below average.

There are on average 0.64 active vehicles per person, against a national average of 0.66.

Around 2.12km per 10k inhabitants of the main city in Pesaro e Urbino consist of bicycle lanes. This makes Pesaro e Urbino somewhat bike-friendly by Italian standards.



The railway that runs down the coast is excellent for exploring the coast places with fast and frequent trains running all day. Busses tend to be rather infrequent, especially if you are looking to explore inland, so hiring a car is essential if you want to explore a bit. The bicycle is perfect to run across the gentle hills and along the beaches.
