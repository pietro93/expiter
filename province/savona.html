Overview

The province of Savona is the 77th largest Italian province by population with 276.064 people, located in the Liguria region.



The larger Savona metropolitan area comprises 69 towns (comuni) and covers an area of 154.629 km2.



The population density is 179 inhabitants per km2, making it somewhat densely populated.



The male to female ratio is 1.00:1.10.



Savona is a coastal city in Liguria, Northwest Italy, the capital of the Province of Savona.



It is the third largest city in Liguria, after Genoa and La Spezia.

Climate

The province of Savona receives on average 228 hours of sunshine per month, or 7.6 hours of sunshine per day.NaN This is 3.39% less than the average for Italy and 2.56% less than the average for the region of Liguria.

Throughout the year, it rains on average 6.29 days per month, which is below average for an Italian province.

During the autumn and winter season, there are usually 0.38 days per month with fog and 0.73 cold days per month with perceived temperatures below 3°C.



In the summer, there are on average 13.56 hot days per month with perceived temperatures above 30°C.

Cost of Living

The average monthly income in Savona is around 1591.19€, which is close to the average for Italy.

The estimated cost of living is around 1890.93€ per month for an individual or 2807.07€ per month for a family of 4.





The cost for renting a small apartment (2-3 bedrooms) in a main city area is around 873.47€ per month.

Overall, Savona is expensive compared to other Italian provinces.

Living in Savona is around 14.59% more expensive than the average of all Italian provinces.

Crime and Safety

The province of Savona is overall moderately safe for expats.



As of 2021, there are an average of 3502 reported crimes per 100k inhabitants.



This is 23.49% higher than the national average.

There have been around 0.2 deadly road accidents and 11.6 serious work-related injuries per 10k people in Savona.



This is respectively 62.96% less driving accidents than average and 10.08% less work accidents than average.

Car theft is reportedly 59.15% lower than average with only 28.81 cases per 100k inhabitants.



NaNReports of house thefts are 66.30% higher than average with 291.05 cases per 100k inhabitants.



NaNCases of robbery are not totally uncommon, around 25.20% higher than average with 27.72 reports per 100k inhabitants.





Savona is considered a relatively safe city with a low crime rate.



Instances of petty theft and pickpocketing can occur, especially in crowded tourist areas, but they are relatively uncommon.
