Crime and Safety

The province of Savona ranks 93 out of 106 for safety according to our data, with a safety score of 3.

There were a total of 4337.585 official reports of crime per 100,000 inhabitants in the province in 2023.

This is in line with the average number of reported crimes per 100,000 inhabitants across all Italian provinces.

This suggests that the crime rate in Savona is typical for Italian provinces.

Thefts and Robberies

In the province of Savona, there were 33.4 instances of thefts with dexterity (thefts committed with special skill, superior to that normally used by the common thief), 35.631 instances of snatch thefts (thefts committed by someone who takes possession of another's movable property by snatching it from the person's hand or body), 37.131 car thefts, and 326.678 home burglaries per 100,000 inhabitants in 2023.
