Overview

This page provides a data-driven overview of crime and safety in Sud Sardegna, in the Sardinia region. Based on official 2023 statistics, Sud Sardegna is one of the safer Italian provinces, ranking 9 out of 106 with a safety score of 8/10.

Below you will find details on thefts, violent crimes, organized crime, drug-related offenses, and accidents, along with answers to common questions about safety in Sud Sardegna.

Crime and Safety

The province of Sud Sardegna ranks 9 out of 106 for safety according to our data, with a safety score of 8. There were a total of 2606.827 official reports of crime per 100,000 inhabitants in the province in 2023. This is in line with the average number of reported crimes per 100,000 inhabitants across all Italian provinces. This suggests that the crime rate in Sud Sardegna is typical for Italian provinces.



Thefts and Robberies

In the province of Sud Sardegna, there were 22.8 instances of thefts with dexterity (thefts committed with special skill, superior to that normally used by the common thief), 10.155 instances of snatch thefts (thefts committed by someone who takes possession of another's movable property by snatching it from the person's hand or body), 64.017 car thefts, and 69.545 home burglaries per 100,000 inhabitants in 2023.
