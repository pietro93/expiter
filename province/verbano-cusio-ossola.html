Overview

The province of Verbano-Cusio-Ossola is the 102nd largest Italian province by population with 157.844 people, located in the Piedmont region.



The larger Verbano-Cusio-Ossola metropolitan area comprises 74 towns (comuni) and covers an area of 226.091 km2.

The population density is 70 inhabitants per km2, making it sparcely populated. The male to female ratio is 1.00:1.07.

Climate

The province of Verbano-Cusio-Ossola receives on average 213 hours of sunshine per month, or 7.1 hours of sunshine per day.NaN This is 9.75% less than the average for Italy and 4.05% less than the average for the region of Piedmont.

Throughout the year, it rains on average 9.54 days per month, which is well above average for an Italian province.

During the autumn and winter season, there are usually 0.85 days per month with fog and 2.77 cold days per month with perceived temperatures below 3°C.

In the summer, there are on average 16.33 hot days per month with perceived temperatures above 30°C.

Cost of Living

The average monthly income in Verbano-Cusio-Ossola is around 1648.31€, which is close to the average for Italy.

The estimated cost of living is around 1714.85€ per month for an individual or 2568.93€ per month for a family of 4.

The cost for renting a small apartment (2-3 bedrooms) in a main city area is around 766.92€ per month.

Overall, Verbano-Cusio-Ossola is expensive compared to other Italian provinces.

Living in Verbano-Cusio-Ossola is around 3.92% more expensive than the average of all Italian provinces.

Crime and Safety

The province of Verbano-Cusio-Ossola is overall very safe for expats.

As of 2021, there are an average of 2287.6 reported crimes per 100k inhabitants.

This is 19.33% lower than the national average.

There have been around 0.7 deadly road accidents and 7.3 serious work-related injuries per 10k people in Verbano-Cusio-Ossola.

This is respectively 29.63% more driving accidents than average and 43.41% less work accidents than average.

Car theft is reportedly 80.19% lower than average with only 13.97 cases per 100k inhabitants.
